A Glance at Trust Through Christian Media

Isayah Ramirez

I’m not entirely sure if I am the only person who does this but moving forward I am just going to assume that I’m not. So the other day I was listening to a mix of songs that I put together to listen to while in the shower. Normally I listen to pop songs and piece together a concert performance where yours truly is the star, but this day I felt a level of conviction towards the music I spend the majority of the time listening to. I decided to switch it up and play some Christian music from an artist that I really have enjoyed recently by the name of Montell Fish. As the songs began to play one track, in particular, caused my concert to stop and be in awe of who God is. The track was called “Heavenly Father,” and in all honesty, there is nothing complex going on in the song, the chorus is “you are my Heavenly Father and I ain’t gotta worry about a thing.” This chorus is a simple truth, that if we trust God, everything else falls in line. That is the common thing I believe that runs throughout Christian media. Trusting in God results in the resolution of what is troubling us. However, instead of saying this outright, Christian media would rather refer to this truth as getting out of a storm, dessert, or deep waters. Nonetheless, trusting in God always results in the same way, the abandonment of control over a situation and leaving it entirely to God. Even the simplicity of the theme of trust in Christian media is mind-blowing. The only reason trusting God is difficult is because we make it difficult. While the song was playing I began to cry with the acknowledgment that I make things harder than they have to be because although it is straight forward, relinquishing control is difficult. I guess that is just the beauty of Christ captured by some media. In order to gain control we must first surrender all control to God.
